Math

Prime Numbers and Patterns

Prime numbers are numbers greater than 1 that have no divisors other than 1 and themselves. Recognizing prime numbers and patterns helps solve problems faster and understand the structure of numbers. For example, 2, 3, 5, 7, and 11 are prime numbers.

Reading

Cause and Effect in Texts

In reading, we focus on cause and effect. For example: "It rained all night, so the streets were flooded." The cause is rain, and the effect is flooding. Understanding cause and effect helps us better predict what might happen in a story or in real life.

Science

Solar System and Planet Motion

The planets orbit the Sun in predictable paths because of gravity. Each planet moves at a different speed and distance from the Sun. Understanding planetary motion helps us know seasons, years, eclipses, and why planets stay in orbit.
